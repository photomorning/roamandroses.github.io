## What Is the Olsen Tuck?

Named for Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen, the "Olsen Tuck" is a deliberately undone styling technique where a top is loosely tucked into trousers or a skirt — never too neat, never fully secured, always suggesting that the wearer has more important things to worry about than perfect symmetry. It's the anti-tuck: casual, slightly rumpled, and endlessly chic.

At the Coach event, Fanning demonstrated the technique in its most refined form. The tuck transformed a straightforward ensemble into something that felt considered but not overthought — precisely the energy that defines modern American sportswear at its best.

## Practical Tips

- The Olsen Tuck works best with slightly oversized tops and high-waisted bottoms — the looseness is what makes it read as effortless rather than sloppy.
- Tuck just the front portion of your top and let the sides and back hang naturally for the most authentic version of the look.
- This styling trick instantly relaxes any outfit, making tailored pieces feel more approachable and rigid silhouettes feel more human.

## FAQ

**Q: What exactly is the Olsen Tuck?**
It's a deliberately casual tucking technique — partially tucking the front of a top into trousers or a skirt while leaving the rest loose — popularized by Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen as part of their signature relaxed-luxe aesthetic.

**Q: Why is Elle Fanning associated with Coach's Spring 2026 show?**
Fanning attended as a prominent front-row guest, embodying the brand's modern, youthful energy and demonstrating styling techniques — like the Olsen Tuck — that align with Coach's current aesthetic direction.
