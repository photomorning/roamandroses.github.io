Sabrina Carpenter Brings Dazzling Nail Jewelry Trend to the 2025 VMAs

When Sabrina Carpenter steps onto a red carpet, she doesn't just arrive — she curates a small cultural moment. At the 2025 MTV VMAs, the Vogue cover...

By June Hart May 21, 2026

When Sabrina Carpenter steps onto a red carpet, she doesn't just arrive — she curates a small cultural moment. At the 2025 MTV VMAs, the Vogue cover star once again proved that beauty lives in the details. This time, the spotlight wasn't solely on her Valentino gown or her feather-trimmed boa. It was on her nails — tiny canvases dripping in jewelry-like shimmer.

Wrapped in a semi-sheer crimson lace dress by Valentino, with a lilac marabou boa draped dramatically across her arms, Carpenter looked like she had wandered out of an Old Hollywood dream and landed squarely in the TikTok era. The dress whispered glamour; the boa shouted playfulness. But it was her bejeweled manicure that sealed the moment.

Nail Jewelry: The Next Evolution

Nail jewelry — a trend that's been bubbling in celebrity beauty circles — isn't about basic rhinestones or a touch of glitter. On Carpenter, each nail became an ornament, catching light like a miniature chandelier. The effect was less "polished" and more "curated," a reminder that nails have graduated from accessory to full-fledged statement piece.

It's a clever beauty strategy, too. Red-carpet looks often risk being swallowed whole by the dress, but by adorning her nails with jewelry-level sparkle, Carpenter carved out her own niche in a sea of sequins and satin. The manicure worked like punctuation on her entire look: precise, unexpected, and impossible to ignore.

The Trendsetter Effect

Fashion critics and fans are already buzzing, with many predicting that nail jewelry could be the breakout beauty trend of the awards season. After all, what's easier to replicate — at home or at the salon — than nails dripping with a little extra shine?

Carpenter isn't new to trendsetting. From her playful coquette-inspired beauty looks to her fearless fashion choices, she's become an unofficial spokesperson for what's next in Gen Z glamour. And if the 2025 VMAs are any indication, the year ahead will be sparkling — quite literally — at our fingertips.

Practical Tips

  • Nail jewelry works best as an accent — let one or two nails carry the sparkle while keeping the rest understated.
  • For at-home nail jewelry, look for adhesive gems or pre-made nail art stickers that don't require professional application.
  • Pair jeweled nails with simple jewelry elsewhere — let the manicure be the star.

FAQ

Q: What is nail jewelry? Nail jewelry refers to three-dimensional embellishments applied to nails — including tiny charms, chains, pearls, and gemstones — that elevate a manicure from simple polish to a sculptural accessory.

Q: How can I recreate Sabrina Carpenter's VMAs nail look at home? Start with a clean, neutral base color, then apply adhesive gems, micro-pearls, or metallic accents using nail glue. Focus on one or two accent nails per hand for an elevated, wearable result.

Q: Why is nail jewelry trending in 2025? As red-carpet beauty becomes more detail-oriented, nails have emerged as a new frontier for self-expression. Jewelry-like embellishments add dimension and catch light in photographs, making them ideal for events and social media alike.
