Greta Lee Brings Old Hollywood Glamour to Her First Venice Film Festival

By Ari Lane May 21, 2026

hero: true heroOrder: 4

For most actors, premiering a single film at the Venice Film Festival would be a career milestone. For Greta Lee, this week felt like something lifted from a dream. Not only is she making her Venice debut — she's stepping into the spotlight with two highly anticipated films, a feat that feels almost cinematic in itself.

Lee, known for her sharp performances and magnetic screen presence, arrived in the romantic canalside city to promote Kathryn Bigelow's A House of Dynamite, where she stars alongside Idris Elba and Rebecca Ferguson. She's also part of Kent Jones's Late Fame, acting opposite Willem Dafoe. For an actor who once described her career as a steady climb rather than an overnight explosion, the moment is nothing short of extraordinary.

A Nod to Katharine Hepburn

On the red carpet, Lee leaned into classic Old Hollywood energy, drawing comparisons to Katharine Hepburn — a blend of tailored elegance and self-assured individuality. At a festival that thrives on spectacle, her look felt refreshingly timeless: a reminder that true style isn't about chasing trends but channeling authenticity with confidence.

Why This Year Matters

Venice has long been a stage for industry-defining moments. For Lee, the dual premieres signal more than red-carpet glamour — they cement her place among the next wave of actors capable of carrying films with both critical weight and global appeal. The festival has launched countless Oscar campaigns, and with Lee at the center of two buzzy projects, industry insiders are paying close attention.

From Brooklyn to the Canals

What makes this chapter even more remarkable is Lee's personal trajectory. Known for balancing indie credibility with mainstream recognition, she embodies the kind of versatility Hollywood craves but rarely nurtures. Seeing her ascend at Venice feels less like a surprise and more like destiny finally catching up.

Practical Tips

  • Old Hollywood glamour translates beautifully to any formal event — focus on clean tailoring, classic silhouettes, and understated confidence.
  • When promoting multiple projects simultaneously, build a cohesive style narrative rather than jumping between disconnected looks.
  • The most memorable red-carpet moments come from authenticity — dress like the best version of yourself, not someone else's idea of glamour.

FAQ

Q: Which films did Greta Lee premiere at the Venice Film Festival? She premiered Kathryn Bigelow's A House of Dynamite (co-starring Idris Elba and Rebecca Ferguson) and Kent Jones's Late Fame (co-starring Willem Dafoe).

Q: Why was Katharine Hepburn referenced in relation to Greta Lee's style? Lee's tailored, timeless elegance on the red carpet drew immediate comparisons to Hepburn's signature blend of sharp tailoring, confident individuality, and old-school Hollywood glamour.

Q: Why is the Venice Film Festival significant for actors? Venice is one of the world's oldest and most prestigious film festivals, frequently serving as a launchpad for Oscar campaigns and industry-defining career moments.
