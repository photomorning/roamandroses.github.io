Ranch-Core Glamour: How Kendall Jenner and Gigi Hadid Made Western Style Fashion's Boldest Trend

By Nina Vale May 21, 2026

"I fully believe I was a cowgirl in a past life," Kendall Jenner admits — a playful confession that captures something surprisingly real about fashion's current mood. Western style, once confined to country music festivals and themed parties, has galloped onto high-fashion runways and celebrity street style with a confidence that feels both nostalgic and entirely fresh.

The Rise of Ranch-Core

Ranch-core — a term that emerged to describe the fusion of Western Americana with contemporary fashion — isn't about literal cowboy costumes. It's about attitude: weathered leather, worn-in denim, artisanal craftsmanship, and a rugged individualism that stands in deliberate contrast to the polished, hyper-curated aesthetics dominating social media. Jenner and Gigi Hadid have become the movement's unofficial ambassadors, translating ranch influences into looks that feel elevated rather than kitschy.

Key Elements of the Look

Western Boots: Statement boots in worn leather or snakeskin, worn with everything from denim shorts to slip dresses.

Denim on Denim: The Canadian tuxedo, reimagined — darker washes, relaxed fits, and an unapologetic commitment to the double-denim silhouette.

Leather and Suede: Fringe jackets, tailored blazers, and accessories that evoke craftsmanship rather than costume.

Earthy Palettes: Browns, tans, deep burgundies, and dusty blues that ground the look in the natural world.

Why It's Resonating Now

Ranch-core arrives at a moment of cultural fatigue with digital perfection. After years of filtered, algorithm-optimized aesthetics, there's a genuine appetite for something that feels tangible, weathered, and real. Western style offers that: it's clothing that looks better with age, that tells a story, that connects to landscape and labor rather than likes and shares.

Practical Tips

  • Start with one Western piece — a pair of quality leather boots or a suede jacket — and build outfits around it rather than going head-to-toe theme.
  • Mix Western elements with modern basics: boots with tailored trousers, a leather belt over a minimalist dress.
  • Invest in craftsmanship; the appeal of Western style lies in quality materials and construction, not fast-fashion approximations.

FAQ

Q: What is ranch-core fashion? Ranch-core blends Western Americana influences — leather, denim, boots, earthy palettes — with contemporary fashion sensibilities to create looks that feel rugged, authentic, and elevated.

Q: How do you wear Western boots without looking like a costume? Pair them with modern, understated pieces — straight-leg jeans and a simple tee, a slip dress, or tailored trousers. Let the boots be the statement rather than piling on additional Western references.
