## Practical Tips

- Nail jewelry works best as an accent — let one or two nails carry the sparkle while keeping the rest understated.
- For at-home nail jewelry, look for adhesive gems or pre-made nail art stickers that don't require professional application.
- Pair jeweled nails with simple jewelry elsewhere — let the manicure be the star.

## FAQ

**Q: What is nail jewelry?**
Nail jewelry refers to three-dimensional embellishments applied to nails — including tiny charms, chains, pearls, and gemstones — that elevate a manicure from simple polish to a sculptural accessory.

**Q: How can I recreate Sabrina Carpenter's VMAs nail look at home?**
Start with a clean, neutral base color, then apply adhesive gems, micro-pearls, or metallic accents using nail glue. Focus on one or two accent nails per hand for an elevated, wearable result.

**Q: Why is nail jewelry trending in 2025?**
As red-carpet beauty becomes more detail-oriented, nails have emerged as a new frontier for self-expression. Jewelry-like embellishments add dimension and catch light in photographs, making them ideal for events and social media alike.
